# Institutional Policy and Compliance Manual

This manual contains the official policies, rules, and guidelines governing operations, academic sessions, student conduct, and infrastructure management.

---

## 1. Code of Conduct & General Discipline

### Primary Objective
The Code of Conduct outlines the ethical, academic, and behavioral standards expected from all students and stakeholders. It serves as a regulatory framework to maintain institutional integrity and harmony.

### Core Working Hours
The standard working hours for academic sessions require students to be present inside classes during designated hours. Academic sessions run from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM daily.

### Attendance Tracking
Students must maintain minimum prescribed attendance levels calculated through institutional logging records. Biometric or manual log registers are used to track entry and exit, which are compiled monthly by the administration.

### Political Activities & Banners
Political activities, unauthorized banners, and strikes are strictly prohibited inside the college campus premises. Engaging in unauthorized political protests or putting up unapproved signage will lead to immediate disciplinary inquiry.

### Property Damage Disciplinary Action
Any damage caused to institutional property will attract strict financial penalties and suspension based on inquiry. The cost of repair or replacement will be recovered directly from the offending students.

## 2. Divyangjan Policy

### Accessibility Infrastructure
The institution provides ramp access, specialized restrooms, and structural infrastructure modifications for differently-abled individuals. All academic blocks are designed with elevators and tactile paths.

### Screen-Reading Software
Yes, IT-enabled support centers provide screen readers and accessible learning materials under the Divyangjan assistance protocol to assist visually impaired students in accessing course materials.

### Exam Scribes & Extra Time
Eligible differently-abled students are granted permission to use scribes along with compensatory extra time during examinations. A request must be submitted to the Controller of Examinations at least 7 days prior.

### Welfare Implementation Monitoring
The Internal Quality Assurance Cell (IQAC) along with the infrastructure committee ensures compliance with accessibility rules. They conduct bi-annual reviews of campus facilities.

### Financial Support & Concessions
Welfare-driven financial aids and assistive device support mechanisms are provisioned under specific student assistance schemes. Does the college offer fee concessions or scholarships for differently-abled students? Yes, selected concessions are approved on a case-by-case basis.

## 3. Anti-Ragging Policy

### Penalties for Ragging
Ragging is a criminal offense leading to immediate expulsion, suspension, and forwarding of the case to local police authorities. The institution maintains a zero-tolerance policy.

### Anti-Ragging Committee Leadership
The Principal heads the Anti-Ragging Committee along with faculty representatives, civil administration, and police officials.

### Complaint Ingress Channels
How can a victim lodge an anonymous complaint against ragging? Complaints can be dropped into dedicated anti-ragging boxes, reported to squad members, or submitted via the institutional portal.

### Statutory Affidavits
Yes, both students and parents must sign and execute statutory anti-ragging affidavits at the time of enrollment. This is a mandatory requirement for completing the admission process.

### Squad Duties
The squad conducts surprise raids across hostels, canteens, and recreational areas to prevent any behavioral misconduct. They submit daily logs to the Anti-Ragging Committee.

## 4. Discipline & Grievance Redressal Policy

### Grievance Redressal Cell
The cell evaluates academic, financial, or administrative complaints raised by students to ensure a fair and timely resolution.

### Resolution Timelines
Grievances must be investigated and resolved within the stipulated statutory timeline specified by the committee router, which is generally 15 working days from submission.

### Parental Ingress & PTA Review
Parents can submit structural feedback or appeals during designated open hours or scheduled PTA review meets. They are not permitted to submit direct grievances on behalf of students during active academic sessions without prior permission.

### Final Arbitration Authority
The Governing Body and the Principal hold the absolute authority for final arbitration on unresolved disputes.

### Sexual Harassment Redressal & ICC
The Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) handles all complaints under strict confidentiality matching the POSH framework standards. The committee includes external experts and legal advisors.

## 5. Infrastructure & IT-Cyber Security Policy

### Wi-Fi Network Utilization
The institutional Wi-Fi network must strictly be utilized for academic purposes; accessing restricted or unauthorized portals is banned. Users are authenticated using unique domain credentials.

### Lab Infrastructure Maintenance
The Infrastructure and Maintenance Committee evaluates resource conditions and coordinates annual repair workflows.

### Software Whitelist Policy
No, any software injection outside the designated IT supervisor whitelist is tracked as a security breach. Installing unauthorized software on laboratory computers is strictly prohibited.

### Hardware Lifecycle replacements
Outdated equipment undergoes scheduled audit classification, asset decommissioning, and structured e-waste management disposal.

### Network Monitoring
Network traffic logging, firewall security rule configurations, and centralized domain access controllers are active. All activities are monitored by the IT Security Department.

## 6. Examination Policies (Internal & University)

### Minimum Exam Attendance
Students must achieve a strict minimum of 75% attendance across courses to be certified eligible for university exams. No exceptions are granted except for validated medical cases approved by the Principal.

### Internal Assessment Marks Computation
Internal evaluations fuse weightage components spanning periodic test scores, descriptive assignments, and session attendance metrics.

### Medical Emergencies & Re-tests
On submission of validated medical certificates, the department provisions specific re-test validation cycles. The certificates must be certified by a registered medical practitioner.

### Examination Malpractice Penalty
Malpractice leads to automatic cancellation of the paper, barring from subsequent exams, and forwarding to the university disciplinary board.

### Exam Sheet Storage & Security
The Chief Superintendent of Examinations along with the designated exam cell controllers secures confidential materials. Evaluation sheets are printed and stored in secure rooms with double locks.

## 7. Mentoring & Placement Cell Policies

### Mentoring Ratio
A specified cluster of students is assigned to a dedicated faculty mentor to monitor psychological and academic progress. The standard ratio is 1:20 (one mentor per twenty students).

### Mentoring Sessions Frequency
Mentors must execute tracking checkpoints periodically each semester, maintaining updated student metric ledgers.

### Placement Cell Eligibility
The placement node maps cgpa cutoffs, active backlog tracking counts, and professional skill metrics.

### Internship Certificates Requirements
Yes, completing designated industrial training workflows is prerequisite to entering recruitment drives. Students must submit valid certificates before placement registrations.

### Soft Skills Training
The Placement Cell collaborates with specialized training vendors to execute corporate alignment workshops.

## 8. Student Assistance & Welfare Schemes

### Vincent De Paul Scheme Focus
The scheme targets socio-economically vulnerable student groups to provision educational subsidies and resource distributions.

### Selection Audits
An income transparency audit and family tracking metrics are verified by the welfare evaluation board.

### Academic Awards & Endowment
Endowment allocations are locked to track top-ranking students across specific academic domain concentrations.

### Financial Audits
Yes, all institutional fund management flows undergo strict regulatory oversight and external auditing checks annually.

### Scholarship Stacking Prohibition
No, policy parameters prohibit stacking multiple internal financial aids to guarantee fair distribution of welfare funds.

## 9. Canteen Management, Operations & Auditing

### Waste Segregation
The paper & plastic wastes of the canteen are separately segregated and handed over to Kudumbasree workers.

### Canteen Water Recycling
The kitchen water collected is used to water the college vegetable garden.

### Budget Preparation
The canteen supervisor is responsible for preparing the annual budget.

### Food Waste Disposal
The food wastes from the canteen are fed to the biogas plant of the college.

### Financial Review
The Bursar is to review expenditure and cash flow once a term.

### Allowed Payment Methods
Payment can be done either as cash or via Google Pay.

### Semester Stock Take
The canteen supervisor will perform a stock take at the end of each semester.

### Operational Audit
The canteen supervisor along with the canteen committee members perform the audit using profit and loss statements.

### Closing of Daily Trading Cashier
All money from the canteen will be counted and signed for by two people at the close of trading each day.

### Canteen Financial Logs
Accurate records are to be kept of money received and expended by way of the college's financial data package.
